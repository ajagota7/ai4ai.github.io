Artificial Intelligence for Actionable Intelligence

Developing trustworthy AI systems for healthcare through causal inference, reinforcement learning, and interpretability

About the Lab

The AI4AI Lab at Imperial College London, led by Dr. Sonali Parbhoo, focuses on developing reliable and interpretable machine learning methods for healthcare applications. Our research combines causal inference, reinforcement learning, uncertainty quantification, and interpretability to create AI systems that can be safely deployed in high-stakes medical decision-making contexts.

We work at the intersection of machine learning theory and clinical practice, ensuring our methods are both mathematically rigorous and practically useful for healthcare providers and patients.

Research Areas

Causal Inference

Developing methods to identify causal relationships from observational data, enabling better treatment effect estimation and counterfactual reasoning in healthcare settings.

Reinforcement Learning

Building safe and efficient RL algorithms for sequential decision-making in medical treatment optimization, including personalized treatment strategies and adaptive clinical trials.

Uncertainty Quantification

Creating Bayesian and probabilistic methods to quantify model uncertainty, crucial for safe deployment of AI systems in clinical environments where confidence matters.

Interpretability

Designing interpretable machine learning models that provide transparent and actionable insights to clinicians, ensuring AI recommendations can be understood and validated.
